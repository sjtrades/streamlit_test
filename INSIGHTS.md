# LILA BLACK - Gameplay Insights

## Insight 1 - Combat Encounters Cluster Around High Traffic Areas

### What caught my eye in the data

Kill and death heatmaps frequently overlap with regions exhibiting high player movement density.

### Evidence

Kill Zones and Death Zones consistently appear near compounds, intersections, and heavily traversed routes highlighted in the High Traffic heatmaps.

### Actionable Insights

Potential actions:

- Adjust cover placement in combat-heavy areas.
- Revisit loot density to reduce excessive early-game engagements.
- Introduce additional escape routes from common engagement locations.

Potential metrics affected:

- Average survival time
- Kill distribution
- Combat frequency
- Player retention

### Why a Level Designer Should Care

Understanding where players consistently engage helps designers create more balanced combat spaces and improve encounter quality.



## Insight 2 - Certain POIs Dominate Match Flow

### What caught my eye in the data

Some Points of Interest attract significantly more player activity than others.

### Evidence

Examples include:

- Mine Pit in Grand Rift
- Central compounds in Ambrose Valley
- Facilities and warehouse areas in Lockdown

These locations consistently appear as bright clusters in traffic, kill, and death heatmaps.

### Actionable Insights

Potential actions:

- Revisit loot density within highly populated POIs.
- Enhance rewards in underutilized regions.
- Adjust spawn distribution to better spread players across the map.

Potential metrics affected:

- Exploration rate
- Player distribution
- Encounter density
- Session engagement

### Why a Level Designer Should Care

Overly dominant POIs can make large portions of a map feel irrelevant. Understanding player attraction patterns helps designers create healthier gameplay flow.



## Insight 3 - Match Playback Highlights Different Player Behaviors

### What caught my eye in the data

Playback reveals noticeable differences in movement patterns between players and bots.

### Evidence

Human trajectories generally exhibit greater exploration and path variation, while bots tend to follow shorter and more predictable routes.

### Actionable Insights

Potential actions:

- Tune bot navigation behavior to better mimic player movement.
- Identify situations where bots appear overly deterministic.
- Improve AI path selection around high-traffic regions.

Potential metrics affected:

- Bot engagement quality
- Player perception of AI difficulty
- Average encounter duration

### Why a Level Designer Should Care

Playback enables designers to inspect individual matches in detail, validate AI behavior, understand player decision-making, and identify opportunities to improve gameplay flow.
